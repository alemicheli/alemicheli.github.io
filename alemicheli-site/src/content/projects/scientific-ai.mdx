## Direction

Scientific data is small, messy, expensive, and high-stakes. The models that work for web-scale benchmarks often fail here. Scientific AI is about methods whose mathematical structure, data efficiency, and reliability are good enough that scientists and clinicians will actually use them.

## What I build

- Survival and risk models for clinical decision support, with calibrated outputs.
- Inference systems for scientific inverse problems.
- Methods that combine domain structure with modern AI to enable discovery, not just prediction.

## Why it matters

The most valuable place for modern AI is not where data is cheap, but where decisions are expensive — diagnosis, design, discovery. That is where careful systems pay off.
