## Direction

Generative models — diffusion, flow-matching, autoregressive — are some of the most expressive priors we have over images, signals, molecules, and scientific fields. The interesting question is no longer *can they generate* but *how do we design generative models that solve real problems*: posterior sampling, inverse problems, conditional reconstruction, and inference under non-trivial measurement processes.

## What I build

- Diffusion-based posterior samplers for inverse problems with general (non-Gaussian) likelihoods.
- Generative models tailored to structured scientific data.
- Inference pipelines that turn expressive priors into reliable decision tools.

## Why it matters

Bridging modern generative priors with the messy realities of measurement is what turns generative AI from a demo into a piece of scientific infrastructure.
